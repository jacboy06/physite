Your New Home

Once you arrive on Osiris, you may notice some subtle differences in your new home. First, the year on Osiris is only 341 days long, rather than 365. Second, the gravity is 3.3% stronger, which means you may register a couple extra pounds on the scale. The planet has a thick and colorful atmosphere, so any horizon will appear closer than it would on Earth. The higher oxygen content makes it easier to breath on the surface. It won't take long for you to get used to the extra gravity, which means you will be able to dive right in to the new culture. As this is one of the later colonization trips, food and infrastructure will have already been established for your convenience. Unfortunately, there are not many plants or animals aside from those we have taken with us. Luckily, all current lodging on the ship is designed to disconnect and become your new home. Despite being already established, the colonies are under the rule of waste not, want not, as there is still a scarcity of basic resources, and only what luxeries we bring. Some down-time activities include rover races, geode collecting, and database reading. Although it may seem bleak, it won't always be for long, and you will always be able to help mold the colonies into a functioning outpost for the human race.

Calculations

Habitable Zone = sqrt(luminosity/1.1) to sqrt(luminosity/0.53) // Luminosity = 12 solar units // Habitable Zone = 3.3 AU to 4.76 AU

Period of Orbit = 2 * pi * sqrt((radius of orbit)3/(contant of Gravity * Mass of Star)) // radius = 5.984*1011 Mass of Star = 3.481*1030 Constant of Gravity = 6.67*10-11 // Period of Orbit = 30378928 sec = 0.963 years = 341 days

Acceleration due to Gravity = Constant of Gravity * Mass of Planet / (radius of planet)2 // radius = 9.284*107 Mass of Planet = 1.31*1027 // Acceleration due to Gravity = 10.14 m/s2

Velocity in Space = Constant of Gravity * Mass of Star / radius of orbit // radius = 9.284*107 Mass of Star = 3.481*1030 // Velocity in Space = 123809 m/s = 123.81 km/s
